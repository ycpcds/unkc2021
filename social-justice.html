Digital Exclusion and the Digital Divide

Global Digital Exclusion

UNICEF: Two thirds of the world’s school-age children – or 1.3 billion children aged 3 to 17 years old – do not have internet connection in their homes, according to a new joint report from UNICEF and the International Telecommunication Union (ITU). “That so many children and young people have no internet at home is more than a digital gap –it is a digital canyon,” said Henrietta Fore, UNICEF Executive Director. “Lack of connectivity doesn’t just limit children and young people’s ability to connect online. It prevents them from competing in the modern economy. It isolates them from the world. And in the event of school closures, such as those currently experienced by millions due to COVID-19, it causes them to lose out on education. Put bluntly: Lack of internet access is costing the next generation their futures.”

UNICEF

  • Young people need to learn foundational, transferable, digital, job specific and entrepreneurial skills to compete in the global economy.
  • Digital exclusion perpetuates inequalities already in existence between countries. Children and Young people from deprived backgrounds are falling even further behind.
  • 58 % of school age children from the most affluent households have internet connection compared to 16 % in the poorest globally.
  • Through ‘Reimagine education initiative’ UNICEF aims to give equal access to digital learning.

GIGA

Launched by ITU and UNICEF in 2019, Giga is a global initiative to connect every school to the internet and every young person to information, opportunity and choice. It aims to ensure that every child is equipped with the digital public goods they need, and empowered to shape the future they want. For more information, visit www.gigaconnect.org.
